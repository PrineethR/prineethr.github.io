social cognitive determiners

educational research | behavioral science | pedagogy • 2023

how do environmental and cognitive factors influence academic performance?

introduction

this research project explores the relationship between social cognitive determiners and academic performance. based on bandura's social cognitive theory, which posits that learning occurs in a social context with dynamic interaction between person, environment, and behavior, this study investigates how self-efficacy and environmental conditions impact student achievement.

the primary research challenge centers on objectively determining learner behavior while accounting for numerous variables that affect assessment outcomes. through classroom research in both bengaluru and ahmedabad, this project examines how factors like environment design, mindsets, values, motivation, and self-efficacy correlate with academic performance.

my role encompassed research design, data collection methodology, classroom implementation, and analysis of results to formulate insights for improving educational approaches.

theoretical framework

the research is grounded in social cognitive theory, which emphasizes the critical role of social influence and both external and internal reinforcement in learning outcomes.

perceived self-efficacy
people's beliefs in their capabilities to produce given attainments, which vary across different domains and development paths. these beliefs significantly impact how learners approach challenges and persist through difficulties.
social influence
the unique emphasis on external and internal social reinforcement, where past experiences shape expectations and determine whether a person engages in specific behaviors and the underlying reasons for that engagement.
reciprocal determinism
the core concept that learning occurs through a dynamic and reciprocal interaction between three key subsystems: personal characteristics, behavior, and environmental conditions.
research hypothesis

i hypothesized that factors including interior design and layout of learning environments, operating mindsets, stakeholder value systems, quality of intrinsic motivation, and self-efficacy can be controlled to predict academic performance and growth.

while mindset shifts and value changes may take longer to manifest measurable results, i anticipated significant correlations between immediate environmental factors, stakeholder interactions, personal characteristics, and academic achievement.

research methodology

the study employed both qualitative and quantitative assessments to gather comprehensive data about self-efficacy and academic performance across different environmental conditions.

1
general self-efficacy survey
administered the GSES to measure nine distinct elements of self-efficacy:
  • self-efficacy in enlisting social resources
  • self-efficacy for academic achievement
  • self-efficacy for self-regulated learning
  • self-efficacy for leisure time and extracurricular activities
  • self-regulatory efficacy
  • self-efficacy to meet others' expectations
  • social self-efficacy
  • self-assertive efficacy
  • self-efficacy for enlisting parental and community support
learners responded to statements with teacher assistance on a confidence scale of 0-100, allowing for thorough reflection on their current mindsets and operating principles.
2
pisa testing across controlled environments
selected three high-rigor learners from thakkarbapanagar english school no. 1 and administered PISA tests over three separate days while controlling their experiences from the start of each day until testing began.

the test assessed multiple cognitive dimensions:

  • critical thinking
  • reading comprehension
  • mathematical thinking
  • patience and grit
  • creativity
  • problem-solving

tests were administered under controlled conditions with consistent rules across all three sessions.

3
environmental manipulation
systematically varied three distinct environmental conditions:

day 1: riverfront - parents instructed to be extremely kind and supportive, no criticism or forced activities, testing conducted in a moderately quiet outdoor location with students seated close together.

day 2: office - parents instructed to radically change behavior, burden students with unusual morning work, behave rudely, and withhold breakfast; testing conducted in a large office room with other adults present.

day 3: tea shop - parents instructed to behave normally; testing conducted in a noisy tea shop with poor infrastructure, uncomfortable seating, and significant air and noise pollution.

maintained continuous documentation of experiences and parental communication throughout the study period.

key performance insights
  • unexpected resilience: rohan's performance paradoxically improved on day 2 despite negative environmental factors. upon investigation, he reported using the emotional stress as motivation to prove himself.
  • environmental sensitivity: the tea shop environment (day 3) showed the most dramatic impact on rohan's performance, suggesting physical environment may matter more than emotional state for some students.
  • individual variation: jayraj maintained relatively consistent performance across all three environments, demonstrating higher resilience to external factors.
  • expectation gap: all three students expected to achieve a minimum score of 9/10, but none achieved this target, highlighting an important disconnect between self-assessment and actual performance.

conclusions
and
implications

this research reaffirmed that academic performance is influenced by a complex interplay of factors beyond what is immediately apparent. the experiment demonstrated that environmental conditions, emotional states, and self-efficacy all contribute to academic outcomes, but in ways that are highly individualized and sometimes counterintuitive.

perhaps most significant was discovering how physical environment can dramatically impact performance for some students while others show remarkable resilience across varied conditions. the study also highlighted a critical gap between student expectations and actual performance, suggesting an opportunity to develop more accurate self-assessment capabilities.

these findings provide valuable direction for improving pedagogy, classroom management techniques, and curriculum development. by understanding the patterns of how social cognitive determiners influence performance, educators can create more effective, personalized learning environments that support diverse student needs.
